Passing Presentation

Passing Presentation is a creative and engaging game where players take turns doing one part of a presentation and then pass it on to the next player to continue. The goal is to collaboratively create a unique and humorous presentation without knowing the previous content.

Here are the rules for playing the game:

  1. Prepare the presentation: Decide on the topic of the presentation and the number of slides or segments it will have.
  2. Set time limits: Determine the time limit for each player's segment of the presentation. This could be, for example, 1 minute per slide or segment.
  3. Random player order: Randomly select the order of the players who will participate in the passing presentation.
  4. Start the presentation: The first player begins the presentation with their segment. They have only their segment's topic and no prior knowledge of the rest of the presentation.
  5. Passing the presentation: Once the first player's time is up, they stop wherever they are. The second player continues the presentation from that point, and they also have no prior knowledge of what was presented before their turn.
  6. Continue until all players present: Each player takes their turn, adding their part to the presentation without knowing what others presented.
  7. Be creative and flexible: Players must improvise and adapt their content to make the presentation flow smoothly despite not knowing the whole story.
  8. Keep it light-hearted: Embrace the humor and unexpected connections that arise from the disconnected parts of the presentation.
  9. End of the presentation: The presentation continues until all players have had their turn, and the last player concludes the presentation. The result is often a funny and entertaining mix of ideas.
  10. Optional: Recording the presentation: Consider recording the entire passing presentation for playback and enjoyment later.
